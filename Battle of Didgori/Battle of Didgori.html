Battle of Didgori

    Overview

    The Battle of Didgori was fought between the Kingdom of Georgia and the Great Seljuk Empire on August 12, 1121. It was a decisive victory for the Georgians led by King David IV, also known as David the Builder.

    Strategy

    King David IV employed a strategy that involved positioning his troops on higher ground, allowing them to have a tactical advantage over the Seljuk forces. He also utilized cavalry charges and coordinated attacks to break the enemy lines.

    Aftermath

    The victory at the Battle of Didgori solidified the unification of Georgia and marked the beginning of its Golden Age. It also halted the Seljuk expansion into the region and ensured Georgia's independence.
